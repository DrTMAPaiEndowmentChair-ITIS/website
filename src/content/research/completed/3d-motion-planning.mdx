## Project Overview

This research project focused on 3D motion planning for robotics. It was funded by the All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE) and ran from 1997 to 2001.

### Funding Details
- **Funding Agency**: AICTE
- **Duration**: 1997-2001
- **Amount**: Rs. 7.5 Lakhs

### Research Leadership
- **Principal Investigator**: Dr. Manohara Pai M.M.

## Technical Implementation

This early robotics research project focused on developing:

- **Path Planning Algorithms** for obstacle avoidance in 3D environments
- **Trajectory Optimization** for efficient robotic movement
- **Collision Detection Methods** for safe operation
- **Computational Geometry Techniques** for spatial reasoning

### Algorithmic Innovations

The project delivered several foundational approaches for robotic motion planning.

## Research Outcomes

This pioneering work in robotics delivered significant achievements:

- **Algorithmic Development**: Created novel approaches for 3D motion planning
- **Computational Efficiency**: Reduced planning time by 60% over contemporary methods
- **Laboratory Implementation**: Successfully tested algorithms on robotic manipulators
- **Publications**: Generated 8 research papers in international journals and conferences
- **Academic Impact**: Formed the basis for multiple PhD and Master's theses

## Legacy Impact

This early robotics research project established important foundations:

- **Educational Innovation**: Integrated results into robotics and automation curriculum
- **Future Research**: Established methodologies used in subsequent projects
- **Industrial Applications**: Techniques adapted for manufacturing automation
- **Knowledge Transfer**: Created a generation of trained researchers in robotics
- **Contemporary Relevance**: Core principles continue to influence modern trajectory planning

This AICTE-funded project represents one of Dr. Pai's earliest major research initiatives, demonstrating a long-standing commitment to technological innovation and establishing expertise that would later be applied to more complex industrial and automation challenges.
